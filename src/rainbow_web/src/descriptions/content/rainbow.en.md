# What the simulation shows

In the final module, droplet physics is connected to an entire rain shower. Each droplet contributes the color that matches the viewing angle between the sun, the droplet, and the observer.

![Droplets at the right viewing angle shine most strongly and together build the rainbow.](rainbow-overview.svg)

# Physics ideas

For an observer near the ground, the primary rainbow lies roughly around $42^\circ$ from the antisolar point, with some variation between colors.

$$
\theta_{\text{rainbow}} \approx 42^\circ
$$

# Things to try

- Place a few droplets manually and study which ones light up most strongly.
- Then increase the rain intensity and watch a statistical arc emerge.
- Compare how the arc changes when many droplets simultaneously land near the right angle.
