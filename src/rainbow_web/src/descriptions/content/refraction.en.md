# What the simulation shows

When a plane wave passes from one medium into another, the wave speed changes. In the simulation you can see the wavefronts rotate as they cross the tilted boundary.

![An incoming wave meets a tilted boundary. The dashed line shows the normal.](refraction-overview.svg)

# Physics ideas

Snell's law connects the angle of incidence to the angle of refraction. If the refractive index increases, light travels more slowly and bends toward the normal.

$$
n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)
$$

# Things to try

- Keep the boundary angle fixed and increase the refractive index.
- Then change the boundary angle and notice that the normal rotates with the surface.
- Compare small and large angle changes to see when the refraction becomes most visible.
