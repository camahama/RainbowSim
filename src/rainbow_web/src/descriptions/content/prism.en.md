# What the simulation shows

The prism module compares air, a straight block, a rotated block, and a triangular prism. This makes it clear that dispersion requires both different light speeds and a geometry that sends colors in different directions.

![In a triangular prism, different wavelengths refract by different amounts at each surface.](prism-overview.svg)

# Physics ideas

Dispersion can be written by saying that the refractive index depends on wavelength, so $n = n(\lambda)$. Shorter wavelengths often get a larger refractive index in glass and therefore bend more strongly.

$$
n_{\text{violet}} > n_{\text{red}}
$$
